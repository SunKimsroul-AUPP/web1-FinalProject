Chronology

A Thousand Years of Khmer Legacy

Follow the historical timeline from early temple foundations to the peak and transformation of Angkorian civilization.

Timeline

A Thousand Years of Glory

  1. 6th-8th Century

    Pre-Angkorian Era

    The earliest Hindu temples emerged under the Chenla kingdom, including Sambor Prei Kuk Cambodia's oldest temple complex.

  2. 9th Century

    Birth of Angkor

    King Jayavarman II declared independence on Phnom Kulen in 802 CE, founding the Khmer Empire and beginning the Angkorian age.

  3. 10th-11th Century

    Temple Mountains Rise

    Massive temple-mountains like Bakong, Pre Rup, and Ta Keo were built as symbolic representations of Mount Meru.

  4. 12th Century

    The Golden Age

    Suryavarman II built Angkor Wat and Jayavarman VII constructed Bayon, Ta Prohm, and Preah Khan, the empire's grandest monuments.

  5. 13th-15th Century

    Twilight and Legacy

    The empire gradually declined, but its temples endured reclaimed by jungle, rediscovered by explorers, and now treasured by the world.
