About the Collection

Civilization, Faith, and Design Excellence

Built between the 6th and 15th centuries, Khmer temples were not only places of worship but also symbols of political legitimacy, astronomical alignment, and engineering mastery.

This museum format helps visitors connect temple architecture with history, iconography, religion, and conservation challenges in a clear digital format suitable for learning and tourism planning.

What This Museum Highlights

Religious Transformation

Many temples transitioned from Hindu to Buddhist worship over centuries. This layered religious history is central to Khmer identity.

Architecture and Landscape

Reservoirs, moats, elevated terraces, and cardinal orientation reveal highly advanced planning tied to cosmology and hydrology.
